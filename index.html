Professional Experience

UMBC Bennett Labs

Research Intern

I had the opportunity to work with Dr. Joseph Bennett at UMBC to use Density Functional Theory (DFT) to investigate how sulfur-based pollutants interacted with calcite and alumina surfaces. I performed DFT calculations, collected data, drafted a published manuscript, and gave presentations, which culminated in a junior first place award in chemistry from the Washington Academy of Sciences .

CMU LearnLab

Undergraduate Research Assistant

I worked with Professor Paulo Carvalho under CMU LearnLab to investigate how how using different active learning techniques impacts student learning. I cleaned and transformed student performance data from seven semesters, created data visualizations, and conduct a meta-analysis to identify differences between POE (Predict-Observe-Explain) and a baseline active learning method.

PA 211

Data Science Intern

I supported PA 211's statewide initiative in assisting thousands of people in need of shelter, food, and utilities by developing BI dashboards and data reports in DOMO. I created ETL pipelines using SQL to integrate data from external software, web-scraped data using Scrapy, and developed a multinomial logistic regression model with 75% accuracy in predicting clients' identified needs. It was my first time working for a non-profit and it was a fulfilling experience, knowing that my work could have made an impact on many people.

CMU PeeX Lab

Research Assistant

At the CMU Privacy Economics Experiments Lab, I am working with a small team on a large scale online field experiment that investigates how targeted advertising affects consumer browsing and shopping behavior. We are developing a chrome and email extension that collects hundreds of terabytes of data from over 1000 participants, each assigned to a different experiment condition (control, ad-blocking, or anti-tracking). I am actively developing features and enhancements for the extension such as a data pipeline to collect localstorage data. We are planning to launch the experiment soon and I will be monitoring participants and analyzing the data collected to derive research insights.

Software Engineering Institute - CMU

Data Science Intern

I am working on projects related to insider risk under the CERT Cyber Risk and Resilience division. Insider risk refer to incidents where an employee or trusted individual of an organization harms the organization. My first project involved collecting, interpreting, and visualizing data from 1000+ insider risk court cases in an annual published report on trends in insider risk. Next, I worked to automate data collection from diverse court case documents by implementing NLP methods, including regular expressions and Named Entity Recognition (NER), to extract relevant fields such as insider name, victim organization, and sentences/charges.
